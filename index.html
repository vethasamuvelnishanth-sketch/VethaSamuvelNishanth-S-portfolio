Market Research Projects

Studies focused on how people think, decide, and adopt — not just what the numbers say.

ESG Performance Growth of Indian Companies

Investor perception & signaling

Problem: Investors claim ESG matters, but which aspect actually influences confidence?

Observed: Transparency and consistency in reporting triggered stronger investor response than high ESG scores.

Analysis: Investors preferred reliability and credibility over “best metrics.”

Insight: ESG communication builds trust; companies should prioritize clarity and consistency, not only numbers.

Solar Tracker — Adoption vs Efficiency

Technology adoption & ROI

Problem: A 30% efficiency increase didn’t guarantee real-world adoption.

Observed: Users cared more about cost, maintenance, and payback period than technical innovation.

Analysis: Decision drivers were practicality and financial security, not performance.

Insight: For adoption, technology must be positioned around ROI, reliability, and simplicity.

Flip Debate — Audience Engagement & Topic Segmentation

Engagement behaviour

Problem: Traditional event topics attracted limited participation.

Observed: Future-focused themes (career, work, money, uncertainty) drew significantly more attention than technical topics.

Analysis: Engagement followed personal relevance and perceived benefit, not topic difficulty.

Insight: To reach wider audiences, design communication around motivation and outcomes, not complexity.

Zoho Survey vs Typeform — Completion & User Friction

Tool comparison & completion rate

Problem: Both tools collect responses, but users don’t complete surveys at the same rate.

Observed: Typeform felt visually appealing but slower, while Zoho Survey’s direct flow led to quicker completions.

Analysis: Extra interactions increased friction; simple navigation reduced cognitive effort.

Insight: Higher completion comes from clarity and speed, not aesthetics.
